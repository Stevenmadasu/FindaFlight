## 2. How much time are users spending on my pages?

**Data Analysis:**
Our average engagement time is **0m 49s**. 
- Users arriving via **Direct** traffic spend the most time (avg. 2m 07s), suggesting high intent.
- Users from **Organic Social** (Facebook/Twitter) spend about 17-31s, typically browsing the "Creative Routes" results.

---

## 3. How many people visited my site in total?

**Data Analysis:**
In addition to the 31 new users, we had **8 returning users**, bringing the total active user count to **39**. The returning user rate of ~20% indicates that users are coming back to check for updated flight prices or to finalize their travel decisions.

## 5. Where are my visitors from?

**Geographic Distribution:**
- **Primary Hub:** Iowa City, IA (targeted campaign region).
- **Secondary Hubs:** West Branch, IA and Chicago, IL.
- **International:** Minimal traffic (1-2 users from outside the US).

This geographic focus aligns with our initial marketing efforts directed toward local student groups and regional travel forums.

---

## 6. What devices are my visitors using?

**Device Breakdown:**
- **Mobile:** 65% (primarily iOS)
- **Desktop:** 30% (Windows/macOS)
- **Tablet:** 5%

**Technical Response:**
Given the 65% mobile usage, we prioritized a "Mobile-First" responsive design for the search results, ensuring that the paired flight cards remain legible on smaller viewports.

---

## 📈 Executive Summary

The FindAFlight platform has successfully established its initial user base. While current traffic (31 users) is growing, the engagement metrics are very healthy.

**Key Insight:** Users coming from **GitHub Referrals** have the highest retention rate, spending an average of nearly 2 minutes on the site. This suggests that the technical audience is highly engaged with the project's architecture and "Smart Ranking" system.

**Next Steps:** To reach the 100-user goal, we will pivot our campaign strategy to focus on broad-interest travel subreddits and student travel groups.
